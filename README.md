# Domain

##  Problem Statement
Farmers in India face multiple challenges, including unpredictable weather, crop diseases, lack of real-time information, and limited access to fair market prices. Most existing solutions are fragmented, making it difficult for farmers—especially those with low digital literacy—to effectively use multiple platforms. This often leads to reduced crop productivity, financial losses, and post-harvest waste. Small and marginal farmers are the most affected, as they rely heavily on agriculture for their livelihood. Addressing this problem is crucial for improving farmer income, promoting sustainable agriculture, and ensuring food security.
